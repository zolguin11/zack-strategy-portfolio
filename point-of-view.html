Point of View

Your story doesn't matter unless it's theirs.

Most brands do not have a story problem. They have a translation problem.

They know what they stand for. The messaging is there. The values are there. It all sounds right in a room full of people who already understand it. Then it leaves that room and lands in someone else's world, and nobody cares.

Not because it is wrong. Because it does not matter to them.

Be a mirror, not a movie

People are not looking for your story. They are looking for something that fits into theirs. Does this feel like me? Does this signal who I want to be?

Same brand, completely different meaning depending on who is looking at it. One student choosing a college might care about big sports and big energy. Another cares about outcomes and stability. Another wants close access to professors and research from day one. Same school. Same campus. Completely different realities.

The brands that work do not force their way into attention. They make sense where they show up.

Culture is a bunch of tiny worlds

There is not one culture anymore. There are thousands of small, distinct worlds, each with its own language, references, expectations, and definition of what matters.

The algorithm did not just personalize content. It personalized reality.

So when a brand tries to speak to everyone, it usually ends up feeling vague to most people. “18-35” is not a segment. “Middle-aged moms” is not a segment. “High-income families in the Northeast” is not a segment. Those are shortcuts.

Real segments are built around beliefs, motivations, and identity.

Simplicity still wins

But not the way most brands approach it. You do not simplify the truth. You simplify the entry point.

Each niche gets a different door, but they all lead to the same house. If every message is complex, nothing spreads. If every message is identical, nothing sticks.

Precision is what makes things simple. When you build the right door for the right person, they do not see a brand. They see a solution to their own story.
