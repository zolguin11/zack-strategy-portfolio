# Hi, I'm Zack Olguin.

I graduated from High Point University in 2021 with a degree in Advertising, Public Relations, and Strategic Communications, plus a minor in Philosophy. That combination kind of sums me up -- I'm curious and I like ideas, but I care most about what happens when ideas meet actual people.

After graduation, I spent time in Los Angeles working in sports and entertainment, where I got a firsthand look at how brands, stories, and experiences fight for attention in a very noisy world. From there, my path took me back to my alma mater, where I worked in enrollment strategy with a clear charge: help increase and shape enrollment. That meant understanding the decision journey, identifying where prospective students and families stalled, translating institutional value into audience-specific messaging, and finding ways to move people from interested to committed. That work taught me that you have to understand what people need to believe, feel, or see before they can take the next step. Sometimes the answer is a sharper message. Sometimes it's a better process. Sometimes it's a more honest explanation.

A lot of what shaped me happened outside the traditional office, too. I've led travel and camping expeditions in multiple countries, guided teams through unfamiliar environments, and learned that leadership is often less about being the loudest voice and more about creating enough trust for people to support one another. On Kilimanjaro, I learned that leadership is not always about being the person pulling everyone forward. Sometimes it's about building a team strong enough that they can help carry you, too. In Tanzania, I learned that respect sometimes looks like eating far more goat liver than any one person should because a Maasai elder offered it with hospitality, and someone needed to represent our group well. And at my aunt's 60th birthday party, I learned the hard way that if the audience asks for Stacey Q, you play Stacey Q.

Those moments have stuck with me because they all point to the same thing: people tell you what matters to them, directly or indirectly. The job is to pay attention.

Now, I'm looking for my next big move -- ideally in brand, advertising, innovation, AI, marketing, or experience strategy. I want to help organizations understand people more clearly, communicate with more precision, and build things that feel useful, memorable, and true.
