Thought experiment

The algorithm personalized reality.

Culture is no longer one big room. It is a thousand tiny rooms, each with its own references, language, and definition of obvious.

Place for a collage of micro-cultures, feeds, and language cues

A lot of brand strategy still talks about audiences like they live in the same shared reality with slightly different preferences. I think that is increasingly wrong.

The algorithm did not just personalize content. It personalized what feels normal, funny, urgent, embarrassing, aspirational, and true. That means two people can technically be in the same demographic and emotionally live in completely different worlds.

So the real segmentation question is not “Who are they on paper?” It is “What world are they inside, and what already makes sense there?”
