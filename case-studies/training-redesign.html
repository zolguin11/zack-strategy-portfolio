Org strategy

Training as a growth system, not a compliance task

When training feels reactive, people experience it as punishment. When it builds confidence, it becomes culture.

Place for workshop model, learning journey, or progression map

The diagnosis

The core issue was not simply a lack of training. It was a lack of confidence, practice, and timing. Passive presentations were not giving people enough repetition or enough ownership to feel prepared in real situations.

The feedback pointed toward a deeper cultural problem: training had started to feel like information delivery after something went wrong, rather than a professional development system built around growth.

What I proposed

I mapped a more active model: peer observations, role-play for tough conversations, feedback loops after major cycles, breakout strategy sessions, mentorship pods, and tiered tracks for newer and more experienced team members.

One concept reframed training as a progression system: orientation and foundations, knowledge tree development, skill tree development, pressure-test moments, and self-guided exploration. The point was to make growth visible and repeatable.

What it shows

This work sits at the intersection of insight, systems thinking, and learning design. It takes qualitative frustration and turns it into a more useful operating model for team capability.
